# <a name="expressions"></a><span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-103">Espressioni</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-103">Expressions</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-104">Le *espressioni* sono costituite da *operandi* e *operatori*</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-104">*Expressions* are constructed from *operands* and *operators*.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-105">Gli operatori di un'espressione indicano le operazioni che devono essere eseguite sugli operandi.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-105">The operators of an expression indicate which operations to apply to the operands.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-106">Alcuni esempi di operatori sono `+`, `-`, `*`, `/` e `new`,</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-106">Examples of operators include `+`, `-`, `*`, `/`, and `new`.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-107">mentre i valori effettivi, i campi, le variabili locali e le espressioni sono esempi di operandi.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-107">Examples of operands include literals, fields, local variables, and expressions.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-108">Se un'espressione contiene più operatori, la *precedenza* degli operatori determina l'ordine in cui vengono valutati i singoli operatori.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-108">When an expression contains multiple operators, the *precedence* of the operators controls the order in which the individual operators are evaluated.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-109">L'espressione `x + y * z`, ad esempio, viene valutata come `x + (y * z)` poiché l'operatore `*` ha la precedenza sull'operatore `+`.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-109">For example, the expression `x + y * z` is evaluated as `x + (y * z)` because the `*` operator has higher precedence than the `+` operator.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-110">Quando un operando si trova tra due operatori con la stessa precedenza, l'ordine di esecuzione delle operazioni viene determinato dall'*associatività* degli operatori:</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-110">When an operand occurs between two operators with the same precedence, the *associativity* of the operators controls the order in which the operations are performed:</span></span>

* <span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-111">Ad eccezione degli operatori di assegnazione e di Unione null, tutti gli operatori binari sono *associativi a sinistra*, ovvero le operazioni vengono eseguite da sinistra a destra.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-111">Except for the assignment and null-coalescing operators, all binary operators are *left-associative*, meaning that operations are performed from left to right.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-112">L'espressione `x + y + z` viene ad esempio valutata come `(x + y) + z`.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-112">For example, `x + y + z` is evaluated as `(x + y) + z`.</span></span>
* <span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-113">Gli operatori di assegnazione, gli operatori di `??` e di `??=` di valori null e l'operatore condizionale `?:` sono *associativi a destra*, ovvero le operazioni vengono eseguite da destra a sinistra.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-113">The assignment operators, the null-coalescing `??` and `??=` operators, and the conditional operator `?:` are *right-associative*, meaning that operations are performed from right to left.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-114">L'espressione `x = y = z` viene ad esempio valutata come `x = (y = z)`.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-114">For example, `x = y = z` is evaluated as `x = (y = z)`.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-115">È possibile controllare la precedenza e l'associatività usando le parentesi.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-115">Precedence and associativity can be controlled using parentheses.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-116">Ad esempio, `x + y * z` prima moltiplica `y` per `z` e quindi somma il risultato a `x`, ma `(x + y) * z` prima somma `x` e `y` e quindi moltiplica il risultato per `z`.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-116">For example, `x + y * z` first multiplies `y` by `z` and then adds the result to `x`, but `(x + y) * z` first adds `x` and `y` and then multiplies the result by `z`.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-117">La maggior parte degli operatori può essere [*sottoposta a overload*](../language-reference/operators/operator-overloading.md).</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-117">Most operators can be [*overloaded*](../language-reference/operators/operator-overloading.md).</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-118">L'overload degli operatori consente di specificare implementazioni di operatori definite dall'utente per le operazioni in cui uno o entrambi gli operandi appartengono a un tipo struct o a una classe definita dall'utente.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-118">Operator overloading permits user-defined operator implementations to be specified for operations where one or both of the operands are of a user-defined class or struct type.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="6cd0d-119">C# offre diversi operatori per eseguire operazioni [aritmetiche](../language-reference/operators/arithmetic-operators.md), [logiche](../language-reference/operators/boolean-logical-operators.md), [di spostamento e bit per bit](../language-reference/operators/bitwise-and-shift-operators.md), nonché confronti di [uguaglianza](../language-reference/operators/equality-operators.md) e [ordinamento](../language-reference/operators/comparison-operators.md).</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="6cd0d-119">C# provides a number of operators to perform [arithmetic](../language-reference/operators/arithmetic-operators.md), [logical](../language-reference/operators/boolean-logical-operators.md), [bitwise and shift](../language-reference/operators/bitwise-and-shift-operators.md) operations and [equality](../language-reference/operators/equality-operators.md) and [order](../language-reference/operators/comparison-operators.md) comparisons.</span></span>
